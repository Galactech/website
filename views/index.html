Frequently Asked Questions

FTC stands for FIRST Tech Challenge. Teams of primarily high schoolers make robots using multiple skills. These robots will perform a specified task by the organization "FIRST". As teams win rounds, they move up in ranks, aiming for winning the world competition. Teams will be using leadership, coordination, teamwork, software skills, hardware skills, and outreach skills. Through robotics, they will spread knowledge and passion surrounding STEM.

Building a robot is a difficult task, and mirrors a job. The amount of time required varies greatly. This is due to factors like team size, goal, age, and time of the year. It would generally range from 6 hours a week to 60 hours.

Robotics requires the initial skills of leadership, teamwork, and hardwork. As your FTC experience progresses, you will learn hardware and/or coding and/or outreach skills.

Many of the members of FTC have either done some sort of robotics in the past, specifically FLL (FIRST Lego League). If you dont have any experience in robotics whatsoever, this is fine. Refining current knowledge of technology and using logic will equip you with needed skills.

The age requirements for FTC are 12-18. Teams usually have a few adult mentors to guide them. There are also other option for different ages: FRC(14-18), FLL(8-12).
